## verificado en ycombinator.com el 9-may-2026

- **batch abierto ahora**: summer 2026 (s26). deadline on-time fue 4-may. late apps siguen evaluándose, "raras veces dan interview".
- **f26 y w27**: fechas oficiales **no publicadas todavía**. la página dice *"you can also apply to future batches (fall, winter, and spring) now"* — misma form, marcas batch futuro.
- **f26 estimado** (pattern histórico): deadline ~1-ago-2026, programa oct–dic 2026 in-person sf.
- **w27 estimado**: deadline ~1-oct-2026, programa ene–mar 2027.
- yc anuncia oficial ~6-8 semanas antes del deadline. esperar confirmación f26 a inicios/mediados de junio.

## standard deal (sin cambios desde 2022)

- $500k total = $125k safe 7% post-money fijo + $375k mfn uncapped.
- sin fees, sin milestones, pro-rata para yc.
- jurisdicciones elegibles: us, canadá, cayman, singapur. founders latam re-incorporan a delaware c-corp / cayman pre o durante batch.
- mudanza obligatoria a sf por 3 meses durante el programa.

## qué busca yc en 2026 (filosofía actual)

### garry tan (ceo desde 2023)
- *"earnestness > polish."* — la cualidad #1 que repite. critica founders haciendo "finishing school" en sf.
- *"a moat is not a noun. it's a verb. every single insight we have is a depreciating insight."* (citando varun mohan, windsurf).
- *"evals are emerging as the real moat for ai startups."*
- *"boil the oceans"* (nov 2025) — retira el viejo "don't boil the ocean". scope chico = manager-mode.
- *"be truthful and precise about revenue. don't mistake one tier for another."* (abril 2026).
- *"you don't need finishing school. you need to build something great, make your users happy, and have craftsmanship."*
- jerarquía revenue oficial (más débil → más fuerte): loi → gmv → carr → transactional → mrr/arr. nunca llamar transactional a arr.

### diana hu / lightcone
- *"ai should not be a tool your company just uses. it should be the operating system your company runs on."*
- *"burn tokens, not headcount. token maxing over headcount."*
- *"if your api bill doesn't make you uncomfortable, you're not doing enough."*

### jared friedman
- vertical ai agents serán **$300b+ category**. *"every company that is a saas unicorn, you could imagine there's a vertical ai unicorn equivalent."*
- *"agents are the software market. go and build what agents will choose."*

### dalton caldwell (partner emeritus desde jun 2025, pero canon)
- *"just don't die."* (mantra #1)
- *"make something people want."* (motto fundacional, intacto)
- *"if i don't understand what the heck is going on in your application, it doesn't even get to the point where it's like 'should we fund this company or not?'"*
- *"what's the right amount of mrr to get into yc? zero."*
- **5× odds** si hay founder técnico hireable a top yc company.
- ~33% de cada batch ya aplicó antes. reaplicar es la norma.
- tarpit ideas = *"ideas que reciben mucho feedback positivo pero los users no retienen"*. consumer/social coordination apps son los clásicos.
- pivot canónico = *"warmer, not colder. like going home — closer to something you know all about."*

### michael seibel
- *"we love your backgrounds, but we hate your idea. are you open to changing it?"* — la frase más usada en pivots mid-interview (caso onu w23, 12h post-resubmit aceptados).
- *"have you tried b2b?"* — pregunta más reusable.
- consumer ai está revival 2024-2025 — *"this is the moment consumer founders have been waiting for."*

### paul graham
- *"founders matter more than the idea."* (ago 2025).
- *"make something people want — fundamentally advice about being empirical, instead of assuming you already have all the answers."*

## anatomía de la app (rúbrica dalton)

dalton lee preguntándose en orden:
> "who are the founders, what's their backstory... what is their idea... what do they have today right now, is it built, are there users... is there evidence people want this... is there evidence the founders are serious... what's impressive or unique."

luego se cuenta una historia. **test**: ¿se cuenta la tuya en una frase tipo cuento?

### one-liner (sin jerga, sin "platform/disruptive/ai-powered")

ejemplos verbatim de aceptados:
- **dropbox (s07, drew houston solo)**: *"dropbox synchronizes files across your/your team's computers."*
- **cruise (w14)**: *"cruise builds a system that inexpensively turns your car into a self-driving vehicle."*
- **mixpanel (s09)**: *"mixpanel is a business intelligence service that helps improve online companies by tracking user interactions."*
- **paystack (w16, nigeria)**: *"apis and tools to enable merchants accept payments in african markets."*
- **basedash (s20, solo founder)**: *"collaborative database interface for teams."*

regla mamá-5s: si tu mamá no entiende en 5 segundos, reescribe.

### founder bio / "why you"

formato: 1-2 logros cuantificados/concretos. **no** "estudió x en y". **sí** "construí x que hizo y medible".

drew houston dropbox verbatim: *"programming since age 5; startups since age 14; 1600 on sat; started profitable online sat prep company in college... reverse engineered poker sites, wrote a real-money playing poker bot."*

### tracción (3 números)

wau + mom% + paying users / mrr. si pre-launch: 2 experimentos con outcomes. usar jerarquía garry estricta. nunca llamar transactional a arr.

### why now (trigger fechado)

❌ "ai is huge" (vacío)
✅ "costo de fine-tuning bajó 30× en 18 meses"

### wildcard "hacking" question

ejemplo oro de griffero (fintoc, w21, chile):
> *"tienes que sorprenderlos. mi respuesta fue que había hackeado mi sistema nervioso tirándome mucho en paracaídas para perder mis miedos. tienes que pensar que la persona que revisa la postulación ve 20 mil al año y tienes que lograr sacarle una sonrisa."*

### videos (son **dos**, separados)

- **founder video, 60s**: todos en cámara. no pitch. no script. bullets. hook 3s. audio limpio (café = auto-rechazo).
- **demo video, 60s**: screen recording producto funcionando, voiceover. sin slides, sin música.

dalton 2018: *"you would be shocked how many people don't follow the dang directions... we get the 20-minute spirit journey video where i don't even know what they're talking about."*

## interview de 10min (formato 2024-2026)

- **10 min exactos**. zoom default desde post-covid; finalists a veces vuelan a sf.
- **2-4 partners** simultáneos. uno lidera, otros rapid-fire.
- decisión **mismo día** (8:55am – 11:30pm pst). yes = llamada de partner. no = email con feedback.
- *"you should assume during the interview that we have the application that you applied with open on our screen."* — dalton.

### preguntas canónicas (sillings)

apertura siempre: *"what are you working on?"* o *"what does your company do?"*

luego rapid-fire por categoría:
- **producto/demanda**: how do you know customers need this? where do new users come from? what has surprised you about user behavior?
- **métricas**: what are your numbers? conversion rate? churn? cac? asp?
- **modelo**: how will you make money? why is your product priced the way it is?
- **competencia**: who do you fear most? what do you understand that others don't?
- **equipo**: how do you all know each other? who is the boss? who would be your next hire?
- **why-you**: why did you pick this idea? what systems have you hacked?
- **futuro**: if your startup succeeds, what additional areas? **are you open to changing your idea?**

### lo que matan
- *"tengo que pensarlo"* o tardar en responder números.
- pitch en lugar de respuesta directa.
- memorización (dalton: *"if someone spits out this memorized paragraph of text about ai, that's weird"*).
- overconfidence sin self-awareness.
- mentir = descalificación permanente. garry (jul 2025): *"you're gonna go to jail."*

### lo que premian
- respuestas 1-3 frases con datos.
- números exactos no redondeados.
- evidencia concreta: *"we began 3 months ago, launched 2 months ago, growing 35% every week since."*
- decir *"i don't know"* si aplica.

### bus factor canon (solo founder)
> *"i am actively using tools and contractors to move fast. i am entirely open to a co-founder, but my standard is incredibly high. i will only bring someone on if they fundamentally change the trajectory of the business."*

**nunca** decir *"i don't need one"*. mata.

## solo founder + latam playbook

### benchmarks tracción

| nivel | métrica |
|---|---|
| mínimo útil | $1k mrr |
| solo founder convincente | $10k mrr + 20% w/w |
| solo founder no-us benchmark | **$20k mrr** (alice.tech w25, copenhagen) |

### casos latam públicos

- **salvy (w24, brasil)**: 4 aplicaciones, ~2 años. única latam w24. ex-ebanx (endeavor). *"if you have a company, just launch as fast as you can on launch yc."*
- **fintoc (w21, chile, griffero+zorich)**: tasa aceptación 1.8%, 7 ensayos en 1 semana, slack en inglés pre-interview. *"durante 10 minutos te bombardean con preguntas donde no puedes respirar."*
- **inevent (s19, brasil, pedro goes)**: $3k mrr al aplicar. partners: kevin hale (1ra) + jared friedman (yes call). sistema tab-shoulder entre cofounders.
- **brainhi (s19, puerto rico, oquendo)**: primera pr en yc. *"the first question on the first interview was 'what do you do.'"*
- **nuvocargo (w18, ecuador, deepak chhugani solo no-técnico)**: jared friedman lo bancó cuando todos los demás partners odiaban la idea. pivoteó post-yc a freight forwarding mx-us.
- **belvo (w20, españa, viguera)**: *"the key to understand the application form is that less is more."*
- **truora (w19, colombia, daniel bilbao)**: 2× por yc. *"direct answers before storytelling"* — la inversión cultural latina más crítica.
- **glio (s13, brasil, joão de paula)**: primer latam histórico. 3-4 aplicaciones.
- **platzi (w15, colombia, vega+van der henst)**: rechazados primera vez, casi no reaplican.

### inglés no-nativo (lebrun, wit.ai w14, canon)

- *"speak slowly, articulate."*
- *"strong accent ok, but harder to understand."*
- *"expect to be cut short about 10 seconds after you start to answer."*
- *"the partners won't remember 4 things about your company. ever."*
- *"keep smiling. be firm but nice."*
- daniel bilbao: *"learn to tell your story, but go straight to the point. no bs."*

### latam en vacío estructural

- griffero (fintoc): *"si w22 era 8% latam, ahora <1%. latin america is being disproportionately affected."*
- gina gotthilf (latitud): *"latin america is hard, there's a lot of local context that's hard to understand if you don't have a local grasp and strong network."*

## reaplicar es la norma (no excepción)

dalton: *"about a third of last batch were companies that applied already. when we see progress, we're like 'wow, way to go.'"*

- salvy: 4 apps, 2 años
- basedash: 4 apps, 2 ideas
- replit: 4 apps (pg dm pre-aceptación)
- close (steli efti): **7 apps**
- webflow: rechazados → show hn viral → entraron next batch

frase canónica para "have you applied before" (paystack w16):
> *"by the last application, it was just a prototype, now we're already running live transactions and we've released additional libraries."*

3 beats: punto de partida + métricas progreso + insight pivot. **si tu reapply es 95% igual a la anterior, no estás listo.**

## pivots aceptables

### caso oro: onu/loula (w23, mid-interview pivot)

seibel abre con: *"so we love your backgrounds, but we hate your idea. are you open to changing it?"* yc pidió *"resubmit your application with a new idea in 1 week."* aceptados 12h post-segunda interview. el framing aceptable: *"ideas closer to our backgrounds — something in fintech since we'd worked at stripe or developer tools since we're both engineers."*

### tiempo mínimo viable de pivot

| tiempo | caso | outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 1 semana | onu (resubmit) | aceptados |
| 4 días pre-interview | mintlify | aceptados |
| 2 semanas pre-interview, sin tracción nueva | stable | rechazados |
| 3 semanas mid-batch | brex, langfuse | exitosos |
| 6 meses post-batch | lago | $22m series a |

### patrón meta confirmado en 14 casos

**pivot exitoso = a algo que ya hiciste internamente o conoces profundamente.**
- slack ← chat interno de glitch
- discord ← chat interno de hammer & chisel
- brex ← payments (pagar.me, vendido a stone)
- retool ← internal tools que ellos construían
- lago ← billing system de qonto
- twitch ← spinoff vertical de justin.tv

### proxy más reusable de tracción nueva: hacker news #1

- webflow: show hn → #1 todo el día → tens of thousands signed up
- lago: hn #1 por 48hrs (777 points)
- posthog: hn launch + 300 deployments en 2 días

## probabilidad realista (founder solo no-us latam)

base rate w26: ~1%. modificadores aplicados:
- solo founder: ÷5
- no-us / latam: ÷2-3
- founder técnico: ×2
- velocity con ai tooling: ×1.5
- $20k+ mrr: ×3-5
- backchannel real (latitud/endeavor warm intro): ×1.5-2
- match con rfs firmado: ×2

range honesto siguiendo guía a la perfección:
- sin tracción + sin network: **0.2-0.5%**
- $5-10k mrr + rfs match suave: **1-2%**
- $20k mrr + rfs match firme + warm intro alum: **3-5%**
- techo realista: ~10-15% (incluso founders fuertes son rechazados constantemente)

probabilidad acumulada **f26 + w27** (2 shots con tracción extra): **~6-9%**.
